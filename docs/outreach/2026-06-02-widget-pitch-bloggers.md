## Variants by blog niche

### Recipe + festival bloggers (Navratri thalis, Janmashtami sweets, Diwali snacks)
Pitch the **festival widget** specifically. Their content is festival-driven; an "upcoming festivals" embed in their sidebar gives readers a reason to come back.

### Vrat + ritual bloggers
Pitch the **daily panchang widget**. Tithi end-time and Ekadashi/Pradosham dates are the daily question their readers ask.

### Parenting + naming bloggers
Pitch the [Baby Names by Nakshatra tool](https://dekhopanchang.com/baby-names) instead — it's a more direct fit. The widget itself is secondary for them.

### Classical music + dance bloggers
Pitch the **festival widget** with the angle: *"Many of your readers plan recitals around auspicious days — Saraswati Puja, Guru Purnima, Vasant Panchami. The widget keeps these visible without you having to update a sidebar each year."*

### Yoga + Ayurveda bloggers
Pitch the **muhurta tool** (if relevant) + the daily panchang widget for *"the daily ritual rhythm your tradition recommends."* Avoid getting too jyotish-deep here — most yoga readers aren't astrology-literate.

## Influencer-on-Instagram tactic

For bloggers whose primary platform is Instagram (not a self-hosted blog):

- DM, don't email — they live in DMs.
- Keep it ONE message. No "Hey! 👋" + ENTER + "Did you know..." chain. One message, one ask.
- Lead with the post you genuinely loved. Then the ask. 3 sentences total.

Example:
> Hi [HANDLE], I came across your Navratri post — really loved how you walked through each Devi tithi-by-tithi. I'm a developer who built a free embeddable panchang widget (https://dekhopanchang.com/widget) that could sit on your linked website. Want me to send a snippet pre-configured for your city? Aditya.

---

## What NOT to do

- Don't follow them on Twitter / Instagram before reaching out as a manipulation tactic. They notice.
- Don't pitch via comment on their latest post — it reads as spam.
- Don't ask for a backlink in exchange. Ask only for the embed. If they want to write about us, that's their initiative.
- Don't include affiliate / referral language. The widget is genuinely free.

---

## Follow-up — 5 days after no response

Just one follow-up. Bloggers get a LOT of pitches; one bump is fine, two is annoying.

**Subject**: `Re: Loved your [SPECIFIC_POST] — built something you might use`

> Hey [FIRST_NAME], just bumping the embed offer in case it got buried — no worries either way. Aditya.

Three sentences max. If they don't reply this time, move on.
