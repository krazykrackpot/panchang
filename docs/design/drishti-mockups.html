Saturn Drishti — 4 Visual Options

North Indian chart · Saturn ♄ HOUSE 1 aspects houses 3, 7, 10

Option 1

Glowing beams of light

Translucent cones of light radiate from Saturn into each aspected house. The houses fill with soft Saturn-blue. Slow auroral pulse. Most "the planet is shining on this house" read.

Aspected houses fill with cool blue. Beam cones project outward. Slow 2.4s pulse.

Option 2

Constellation threads

Thin gold filaments connect Saturn to each aspected house, with star tips. Aspect number (3, 7, 10) labels each thread. Mystical, restrained, very Jyotish-native.

Three-layer filaments (blur halo → soft glow → gradient core) along gentle bezier arcs · sparkle stars pop in at thread tips with a slow halo ripple.

Option 3

Aspect halos + sigil echoes

Each aspected house gets a colored ring + soft glow + a ghost copy of ♄ in its corner. No lines crossing the chart. Halo intensity = aspect strength (7th = brightest = full aspect).

Clean — no lines cross the chart. Halo brightness encodes aspect strength.

Option 4

Comet trail animation

Three comets fly out in sequence from Saturn to each aspected house (staggered 300ms apart). They land and leave a persistent pulsing star. Dramatic, casts drishti like a spell.

Aspected houses light up with gold boundaries + soft fill · everything else dims back · comets streak through the foreground.

All four work for both North Indian (shown) and South Indian (rectangular grid) chart styles — the visual treatments are independent of layout. The chart base would change; the drishti overlay logic stays the same.
