# SEO Sitemap Audit — 2026-04-16

## Context

Google Search Console reported:

| Bucket | Count |
|---|---|
| Page with redirect | 528 |
| Alternative page with proper canonical tag | 243 |
| Excluded by `noindex` tag | 5 |

## Diagnosis

Sitemap emitted **9,390 URLs** (939 per locale × 10 locales) on a young domain.
Breakdown per locale:

| Segment | Count | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `/daily/*` | 408 | Ephemeral, algorithmically generated; ~43% of the sitemap |
| `/learn/*` | 182 | Real content |
| `/panchang/*` | 100 | Includes ~50 city pages, templated |
| `/matching/*` | 80 | Rashi pair compatibility, partly templated |
| `/calendar/*` | 63 | Festival pages |
| `/puja/*` | 54 | Puja vidhi pages |
| misc | 52 | |

Root causes of GSC buckets:
- **2,874 Discovered, not indexed** = sitemap exceeds crawl budget; Google skips templated duplicates.
- **106 Duplicate canonical** = locale clones of templated pages look identical across cities/coordinates.
- **49 Crawled, not indexed** = quality signals on thin pages.

## Shipped fixes (commit `5e58e50`)

1. **Removed `/daily/[date]` and `/daily/[date]/[city]` from sitemap.** They now carry `robots: { index: false, follow: true }`. Pages still function for direct-nav users; Google stops wasting crawl budget on them. The `/daily` hub remains indexable.

2. **Submit only `en` + `hi` in sitemap.** Other 8 locales remain fully functional and linked via `alternates.languages` on every sitemap entry. Users and Google still know they exist — we just stop *asking* Google to crawl 10 near-identical variants before the domain has authority.

3. **Verified every sitemap route maps to a real `page.tsx`.** Zero dangling routes. The 1 GSC 404 is likely a stale URL from a prior redirect; will age out of Google's index over weeks.

**Result: 9,390 → 1,064 sitemap URLs (−88%)**. Every entry still advertises all 10 languages via hreflang.

## Deferred (do not execute yet)

### Step 4 — City panchang consolidation
Keep top 10 cities in sitemap; noindex the other ~40 with canonical → `/panchang`.

**Risks:** Loses long-tail SEO on smaller cities. Canonical-to-parent can be *ignored* by Google if the city page has distinct content (already seen this in the "Duplicate canonical" bucket). "Top 10" selection is subjective. Some city pages may already rank — **check GSC Performance before cutting**.

### Step 5 — Matching pair consolidation
Keep top ~20 rashi pairs in sitemap; noindex the other 120.

**Risks:** Rashi pair compatibility is evergreen, high-intent search. Unlike daily panchang, each pair has genuinely differentiated content (score, planets, narrative). Matching is the **most commercially valuable** SEO vertical for a panchang app — cut last, not first. Also check whether `mesh-tula` and `tula-mesh` are already deduplicated (that's a 144 → 72 optimization without cutting content).

### Step 6 — `/puja/*` and `/calendar/*` audit
Inspect each; noindex thin ones.

**Risks:** Festival pages are *seasonally* important — a low-impression April doesn't predict an October peak. Puja vidhi pages are high-intent for future monetization. Over-cutting here is costlier than over-cutting steps 4/5.

## Decision: wait and measure

Steps 1–3 already cut 88% of the sitemap. Let them propagate through Google for **4–8 weeks** before doing steps 4–6.

**Cheapest way to turn 4/5/6 from guesses into decisions:**
Export GSC Performance data filtered to `/panchang/`, `/matching/`, `/puja/`, `/calendar/`. That tells us which pages actually earn impressions so we cut surgically, not preemptively.
