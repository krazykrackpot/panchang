# Video Script: Why 7 Days in THAT Order?
## Duration: 2:30 | Language: English | Format: Vertical (9:16) + Horizontal (16:9)

---

### SCENE 1: HOOK (0:00 – 0:08)
**Visual:** Dark background → days of the week appear one by one in gold text, each word pulsing as it appears: Sunday → Monday → Tuesday → Wednesday → Thursday → Friday → Saturday

**Narration:**
> "Sunday, Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday. You say these every day. But WHY are they in this order? It's not random. It's a 4,000-year-old astronomical code — and India cracked it."

**On-screen text:** "WHY THIS ORDER?"

---

### SCENE 2: THE 7 PLANETS (0:08 – 0:35)
**Visual:** Night sky → 7 celestial bodies appear orbiting Earth in concentric circles, from slowest (outermost) to fastest (innermost). Each labeled with orbital period.

**Narration:**
> "Ancient astronomers could see 7 moving objects in the sky — the Sun, Moon, and 5 visible planets. They ranked them by orbital speed, from slowest to fastest: Saturn at 29 years... Jupiter at 12... Mars at 2... the Sun at 1 year... Venus at 225 days... Mercury at 88 days... and the Moon at just 27 days."

**Visual:** Show the orbital speed ladder vertically:
```
Saturn    ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ 29.5 years
Jupiter   ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ 11.9 years
Mars      ━━━━━━━ 1.88 years
Sun       ━━━━━ 1 year
Venus     ━━━ 225 days
Mercury   ━━ 88 days
Moon      ━ 27.3 days
```

---

### SCENE 3: THE HORA SYSTEM (0:35 – 1:15)
**Visual:** A 24-hour clock wheel divided into segments, each colored by its ruling planet.

**Narration:**
> "Here's where it gets brilliant. They assigned each HOUR of the day to one of these 7 planets, cycling through them in orbital speed order. Hour 1 of Saturday — Saturn. Hour 2 — Jupiter. Hour 3 — Mars. And so on... cycling through all 7, three times through 24 hours, with 3 left over."
>
> "Now the key insight: the planet that rules the FIRST hour of the day names that day. Saturday starts with Saturn. Count 24 hours through the sequence... hour 25 — the first hour of the NEXT day — lands on the SUN. That's Sunday."

**Visual:** Animated counting through the 24 hours on the wheel, highlighting each planet. When it reaches hour 25, it glows gold and "SUNDAY" appears.

> "Count another 24... you land on the MOON. Monday. Then Mars — Tuesday. Mercury — Wednesday. Jupiter — Thursday. Venus — Friday. And back to Saturn — Saturday."

**Visual:** The 7-day sequence materializes as a golden ring: Sa → Su → Mo → Ma → Me → Ju → Ve → Sa

---

### SCENE 4: THE SKIP-3 PATTERN (1:15 – 1:40)
**Visual:** The orbital speed ladder reappears. A golden arrow skips every 3rd planet.

**Narration:**
> "There's an elegant shortcut. In the orbital speed list, skip every 2 planets: Saturn... skip Jupiter, Mars... land on Sun. Skip Venus, Mercury... land on Moon. Skip Saturn, Jupiter... land on Mars. The weekday order is the orbital speed order with every 3rd planet selected."

**On-screen text:** "Skip-3 Pattern"
**Visual:** Saturn → (skip) → (skip) → **Sun** → (skip) → (skip) → **Moon** → (skip) → (skip) → **Mars** → ...

## Production Notes

### Visual Style
- Background: Deep navy (#0a0e27) — matches app theme
- Text: Gold (#f0d48a) for highlights, white for body
- Planet colors: Sun=amber, Moon=blue, Mars=red, Mercury=emerald, Jupiter=yellow, Venus=pink, Saturn=slate
- Animations: Smooth eases, no jarring cuts. Celestial/cosmic feel.
- Font: Cinzel for titles, Inter for body, Noto Sans Devanagari for Sanskrit

### Music
- Ambient cosmic/meditative track, soft sitar or tanpura drone
- Build intensity during Scene 3 (the "aha" moment)
- Resolve to calm for CTA
