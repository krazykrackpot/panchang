# 00 — Product brainstorm (before you know what you're building)

Use this **before any code exists.** Output should be a crisp one-page product
spec you can sanity-check with 3 potential users before investing a week of
building.

---

I'm thinking about building `<ONE-SENTENCE HUNCH>`. I don't yet know if it's
worth building, what exactly it does, or who it's for. Be my product-discovery
partner. Don't write code. Don't even pick a stack yet. Push back on anything
that sounds vague.

## Help me answer these, in order

1. **The problem.** In one paragraph, what problem does this solve? Who has
   it, how often, and how much pain does it cause today? Who currently
   solves it (competitors, workarounds, spreadsheets)?

2. **The user.** Name one specific person (real or archetype) who has this
   problem. What's their day like? What do they use today? What would make
   them switch?

3. **The smallest thing that could work.** What's the 2-week version that
   solves ONE real workflow end-to-end — not 10% of ten workflows? Be
   aggressive about cutting scope.

4. **The "do not build" list.** List 10 adjacent features this product
   could have but absolutely shouldn't in v1. For each, write the specific
   reason you'd defer it.

5. **The measurable success signal.** If I launch the MVP on day 14, what
   single number would tell me in 2 weeks whether this is worth continuing?
   Not DAU. Something action-specific: "N users saved a chart and returned
   within 7 days," "N users completed the flow without contacting support,"
   etc.

6. **Distribution plan.** How do the first 100 users find this? Be concrete
   — "Product Hunt launch" doesn't count. Which 3 communities, which 2
   keywords, which 1 friend who will introduce it.

7. **Why now / why you.** What's changed in the world that makes this
   buildable/wanted today that wasn't true 2 years ago? What's your
   unfair advantage vs someone else building the same thing?

8. **Kill criteria.** What would I need to learn in 30 days to convince me
   to kill this project? Write it down now so future-me can't rationalize.

## Before we start

Ask me 5 clarifying questions first. Don't answer any of the 8 above until
my answers to those 5 are concrete enough for you to build a real spec.

If my original hunch is vague, mushy, or sounds like a platform / AI-for-X /
community / "Uber for Y" pitch — challenge me. The best products are
embarrassingly specific.

**Do not write code or pick a tech stack yet.** This is pure product. When
we're done you'll hand me a 1-page spec I can show 3 potential users
tomorrow.
