## Part I — Astronomy (Mathematical & Observational Foundation)

These texts are the scientific substrate of Jyotish. They describe planetary motion, time cycles, trigonometry, and eclipse calculation. Relevant to anyone building or validating ephemeris software.

---

### 1. Vedanga Jyotisha — Lagadha (~1200–1400 BCE)
**Tag:** PD-US · [archive.org — 1985 scholarly edition](https://archive.org/details/VedangaJyotisa) · [1936 Shama Shastry translation](https://archive.org/details/EfNG_vedanga-jyotisha-by-r-shama-shastry-english-1936-united-by-the-asst.-supt.-govt.-branch-press-m)

The oldest surviving Vedic astronomical text and the origin point of Jyotish as a Vedanga (limb of the Veda). Covers tithi, nakshatra, and ritual timing through solar and lunar positions. 36 verses (Rigvedic recension) and 43 verses (Yajurvedic recension). Historically important as the root from which the entire tradition grew.

---

### 2. Aryabhatiya — Aryabhata (499 CE)
**Tag:** PD-US · [archive.org — Shukla & Sarma commentary edition](https://archive.org/details/Aryabhatiya-with-English-commentary)

The earliest surviving complete Indian mathematical-astronomical work. Covers arithmetic, algebra, plane and spherical trigonometry, and planetary calculation in 121 verses. Introduced the sine table and proposed a rotating Earth. The eclipse calculation and planetary period methods here directly inform how Vedic software computes Rahu/Ketu and eclipses. Shukla & Sarma commentary edition (1976) is the scholarly standard.

---

### 3. Panchasiddhantika — Varahamihira (~550 CE) · Trans. Thibaut & Dvivedi (1889)
**Tag:** PD-US · [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/wg1078)

Varahamihira's summary of five earlier astronomical siddhantas: Surya, Romaka, Paulisa, Vasishtha, and Paitamaha. Essential for understanding the diversity of ancient Indian planetary computation schools and how they differ in their mean-planet tables, ayanamsa, and eclipse calculations. Thibaut's 1889 translation is scholarly and annotated. Definitively public domain.

---

### 4. Brahmasphutasiddhanta — Brahmagupta (628 CE)
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org Vol. 1](https://archive.org/details/Brahmasphutasiddhanta_Vol_1)

The major astronomical text after the Surya Siddhanta. Famous for the first explicit rules of arithmetic with zero and negative numbers. Contains detailed chapters on planetary computation, eclipse prediction, and gnomon (shadow) calculations. Relevant for understanding the mathematical framework behind mean planet positions.

---

### 5. Surya Siddhanta (Ancient; trans. Burgess 1858)
**Tag:** PD-US · [archive.org — Burgess translation](https://archive.org/details/SuryaSiddhantaTranslation)

The foundational Hindu astronomical text used as the computational standard across Vedic traditions. Covers planetary mean and true positions, time cycles (Maha Yuga, Kalpa), eclipses, the concept of ayanamsa, sunrise/sunset calculation, and sidereal time. Burgess's 1858 translation with extensive mathematical notes remains the authoritative English version. Definitively public domain.

---

## Part II — Jyotish (Interpretive Tradition)

Divided into six domains: Natal, Predictive/Dasha, Divisional/Jaimini, Muhurta, Prashna, and Nadi.

---

### II-A — Natal Astrology (Janma Kundali)

Core texts for reading the birth chart: planetary natures, house meanings, yogas, and general life results.

---

#### 6. Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra (BPHS) — Parashara / Santhanam trans.
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org — Santhanam (2 vols.)](https://archive.org/details/BPHSEnglish) · [Girish Chand Sharma Vol. 1](https://archive.org/details/brihatparasarahorasastrawithenglishtranslationgirishchandsharmavolume1_547_G) · [Vol. 2](https://archive.org/details/brihatparasarahorasastrawithenglishtranslationgirishchandsharmavolume2_433_w)

**The single most important text in Vedic astrology.** The compendium attributed to Maharishi Parashara covering rashi significations, planetary karakatvas, house lordships, the complete yoga catalog, Vimshottari dasha, ashtakavarga, all 16 divisional charts (D1–D60), and remedial measures. Everything taught in modern Jyotish either derives from or is cross-referenced against this text. Santhanam's two-volume English translation is the standard. The Girish Chand Sharma edition includes original Sanskrit slokas.

---

#### 7. Brihat Jataka — Varahamihira (~580 CE) · Trans. Vijnananda / Suryanarain Rao (1905)
**Tag:** PD-US · [archive.org — Swami Vijnananda](https://archive.org/details/BrihatJatakaOfVarahamihiraBySwamiVijnananda) · [B. Suryanarain Rao 1905](https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.406251) · [Subrahmanya Sastri 2nd ed.](https://archive.org/details/BrihatJataka2ndEd.ByVSubrahmanyaSastri)

The most authoritative pre-medieval natal astrology text, written with mathematical precision by the author of the Panchasiddhantika. Covers planetary natures, house meanings, planetary conjunctions, shadbala (planetary strength), longevity calculation (ayurdaya), and female horoscopy. More concise and technically rigorous than BPHS; used as a check against BPHS interpretations. Definitively public domain in multiple translations.

---

#### 8. Brihat Samhita — Varahamihira · Trans. Subrahmanya Sastri (1946)
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org — with OCR](https://archive.org/details/varahamihira-brihat-samhita-panditabhushana-v-subrahmanya-sastri-1946-with-english-ocr-layer) · [plain scan](https://archive.org/details/VarahamihirasBrihatSamhitaByVSubrahmanyaSastri)

A vast encyclopedic text covering mundane astrology (political predictions from planetary positions), eclipse interpretation, nakshatra character, weather prediction, omens, architecture, and natural signs. Essential for understanding the broader Vedic worldview in which Jyotish is embedded, particularly for mundane and predictive applications. Searchable OCR version available.

---

#### 9. Saravali — Kalyana Varma (~800 CE) · Trans. Santhanam
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org — Santhanam](https://archive.org/details/KalyanaVarmasSaravali) · [alternate](https://archive.org/details/saravali00kalyuoft)

A comprehensive natal chart text covering planetary effects in each of the 12 houses, wealth/poverty combinations, longevity, and character readings. Fills many interpretive gaps in BPHS with explicit sloka-by-sloka results for planetary placements. Second only to BPHS in practical interpretive utility for house lords and planetary combinations.

---

#### 10. Phaladeepika — Mantreshwara (~16th c.) · Trans. Subrahmanya Sastri (1950)
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org — 2nd ed.](https://archive.org/details/Phaladeepika2ndEd.1950ByVSubrahmanyaSastri) · [IGNCA](https://archive.org/details/in.gov.ignca.6945)

A highly respected predictive text covering planetary states (combustion, retrogression, exaltation), dasha results, yogas, and house results in a concise, practical format. Popular in South Indian Jyotish practice. Includes original Sanskrit slokas. Subrahmanya Sastri's translation is scholarly and extensively annotated. Widely used as a practitioner's day-to-day reference alongside BPHS.

---

#### 11. Jataka Parijata — Vaidyanatha Dikshita (~1425 CE) · Trans. Subrahmanya Sastri (3 vols.)
**Tag:** Open · [Vol. I](https://archive.org/details/JatakaParijataVolIOfIIByVSubrahmanyaSastri) · [Vol. II](https://archive.org/details/JatakaParijataVolIIOfIIByVSubrahmanyaSastri)

An exhaustive three-volume work covering ayurdaya (longevity calculation), the complete yoga catalog, house meanings, Vimshottari dasha, ashtakavarga, and female horoscopy. One of the most detailed and technically complete classical texts; Subrahmanya Sastri's translation includes worked examples and extensive comparative notes. Essential for deep-level natal analysis.

---

#### 12. Sarvartha Chintamani — Venkatesa Sarma (~13th c.) · Trans. Suryanarain Rao (1899)
**Tag:** PD-US · [archive.org — Rao collection](https://archive.org/details/Astrology_Books_by_B_Suryanarayana_Row)

One of the most cited classical texts on planetary yogas. An extremely detailed catalog of rajayogas, dhana yogas, and negative combinations — over 2,000 slokas covering almost every possible planetary combination. Rao's 1899 translation is definitively public domain and remains the standard English version. Essential for yoga identification and synthesis.

---

#### 13. Chamatkar Chintamani — Bhatta Narayana · Trans. available
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/chamatkar-chintamani-of-bhatta-narayana-pdf) · [alternate](https://archive.org/details/chamatkarchintamanibrajbiharilalsharma)

A concise text giving specific results of each planet placed in each of the 12 rashis, written in an aphoristic style popular among practising astrologers. Widely used as a quick reference for rashi-based planetary interpretation. Compact enough to be memorised; direct enough to be immediately applicable.

---

#### 14. Hora Sara — Prithuyasas (~6th c. CE) · Trans. Santhanam
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/HoraSaraRSanthanamEng) · [alternate](https://archive.org/details/HorasaraOfPrithuyasasJyotishVedicAstrology)

A 1,209-sloka natal astrology text by the son of Varahamihira. Covers planetary natures, house results, and yogas in a style bridging Varahamihira and the later BPHS tradition. Historically significant as one of the earliest post-Varahamihira texts and as evidence of how the tradition evolved between Varahamihira and the Parashara school.

---

#### 15. Bhavartha Ratnakara — Ramanujacharya · Trans. B.V. Raman
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/BhavarthaRatnakaraByBVRaman) · [DLI](https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.134838)

Organised by bhava (house), giving sloka-by-sloka planetary combinations for each house's significations. Exceptionally practical — used by astrologers as a quick reference for house lord combinations and inter-house relationships. B.V. Raman's translation is clear and accessible even for beginners.

---

### II-B — Predictive Astrology & Dasha Systems

Texts focused on timing of events through dasha/antardasha sequences and transit triggers.

---

#### 16. Laghu Parashari (Jataka Chandrika) — Parashara school · Trans. O.P. Verma
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/LaghuParashariOPVerma) · [alternate](https://archive.org/details/laghu-parashari-o.-p.-verma)

The essential short text focused exclusively on Vimshottari dasha interpretation — specifically house lord results during their maha dashas and antardashas. More compact and immediately applicable than the full BPHS dasha chapters. The standard starter text for learning Vimshottari dasha interpretation; most KP and classical practitioners memorise its core slokas.

---

#### 17. Uttara Kalamrita — Kalidasa · Trans. Subrahmanya Sastri
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/uttkalamrita-kalidas-ps-sastri) · [alternate](https://archive.org/details/uttara-kalamritam.)

Covers karakas (planetary significators) and their role in dasha interpretation; also covers house meanings and yogas. The concept of the Karakamsha (navamsha of the atmakaraka) is elaborated here in detail. Widely cited in Jaimini astrology discussions due to overlap with Jaimini karakatva concepts. Also an important reference for the extended karakamsha interpretation used in this application.

---

#### 18. Deva Keralam / Chandra Kala Nadi — Achyuta & Venkatesa · Trans. Santhanam (3 vols.)
**Tag:** Open · [Vol. 1](https://archive.org/details/deva-keralam-1-chandrakala-nadi_202309) · [Vol. 2](https://archive.org/details/deva-keralam-2-chandrakala-nadi) · [Vol. 3](https://archive.org/details/j-90370-r-santhanam-deva-keralam-chandra-kala-nadi-2004-vol-3)

One of the major Nadi predictive texts (9,181 slokas, fully translated). Organised by Moon-sign and nakshatra, giving specific predictions for planetary combinations. The Chandra Kala Nadi system uses transit triggers over natal positions for event timing — a distinct and complementary methodology to Vimshottari dasha. Three large volumes; Santhanam's translation is the accessible English standard.

---

#### 19. Bhrigu Nandi Nadi — R.G. Rao (Bhrigu tradition compilation)
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/jyotish-bhrigu-nandi-nadi-rg-rao) · [alternate](https://archive.org/details/bhrigu-nandi-nadi)

Key English-language work on Bhrigu Nandi Nadi — a system of prediction based on planetary pairs and their inter-relationships rather than standard house analysis. Uses Jupiter as the primary significator and interprets planetary conjunctions and aspects distinctly from classical Parashara methods. Useful companion for the Bhrigu Bindu concept used in this application.

---

#### 20. Brighu Naadi Sangraha — N. Srinivasan Shastry
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/brighu-naadi-sangraha-n-srinivasan-shastry)

An English compilation of Bhrigu Nadi principles and predictions. Covers the interpretation of planetary position sequences as found in the original Bhrigu leaf manuscripts. Useful alongside the R.G. Rao text for cross-referencing the Bhrigu Nadi tradition.

---

### II-C — Divisional Charts & Jaimini System

---

#### 21. Jaimini Sutras — Maharishi Jaimini · Trans. B. Suryanarain Rao (1902/1955)
**Tag:** PD-US (1902 original) · [archive.org — DLI](https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.486584) · [1955 reprint](https://archive.org/details/Jaiminisutras1955EditionByBSRao) · [Rao complete collection](https://archive.org/details/Astrology_Books_by_B_Suryanarayana_Row)

The foundational text of the Jaimini system — the second major classical Vedic astrology tradition alongside Parashara. The sutras cover Chara Karakas (planetary significators that rotate each chart), Chara Dashas (time periods based on signs), Argala (planetary intervention), Arudha padas, and Karakamsha interpretation. Definitively public domain in the original Rao translation. All Jaimini features in this application derive from this text.

---

### II-D — Muhurta (Electional Astrology)

---

#### 22. Muhurta Chintamani — Rama Daivagya (~1600 CE)
**Tag:** Open · [Kedar Datt Joshi ed.](https://archive.org/details/muhurta-chintamani-kedar-datt-joshi) · [Mahidhar Sharma](https://archive.org/details/muhurta-chintamani-of-daivagya-ramacharya-mahidhar-sharma) · [Govind Jyoti](https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.553968) · [Khemraj 1855](https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.272907)

The standard classical reference for Muhurta — selection of auspicious times. Covers the 28 nakshatra qualifications for each type of activity, tithi suitability, vara (weekday) and hora (planetary hour) selection, Panchaka dosha, and activity-specific rules for weddings, travel, surgery, business opening, and more. Written at Kashi (~1600 CE). All Muhurta AI rules in this application are derived from this text.

---

#### 23. Muhurtha or Electional Astrology — B.V. Raman
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.128092) · [alternate](https://archive.org/details/MuhurtaElectionalAstrologiByRaman)

A practical English-language synthesis of classical Muhurta principles covering nakshatras, tithis, yogas, and specific event elections (marriage, travel, construction, etc.). More accessible than the Sanskrit originals; useful as a companion to Muhurta Chintamani. Raman synthesises multiple classical sources and resolves their contradictions for modern practitioners.

---

### II-E — Prashna (Horary Astrology)

---

#### 24. Prasna Marga — Kerala tradition (~16th–17th c.) · Trans. B.V. Raman (2 vols.)
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org — Raman 2 vols.](https://archive.org/details/PrasnaMargaBVR)

The most comprehensive classical text on horary astrology, from the Kerala Jyotish tradition. Covers Prashna chart casting, Arudha lagna, Prana/Deha/Mrityu lords, omens (Shakuna), lost articles, illness, travel, and death timing. B.V. Raman's translation includes the original Malayalam-Sanskrit text with extensive commentary. The reference text for all Prashna features in this application.

---

#### 25. Prasna Tantra — B.V. Raman
**Tag:** **CC0** · [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/UJrg_prasna-tantra-by-b.-v.-raman-raman-publication)

Raman's systematic treatment of horary astrology covering Prashna chart casting, house significators, and event timing. More systematic than Prasna Marga for modern practitioners. The CC0 public domain dedication makes this the legally cleanest modern text in the collection — suitable for self-hosting without qualification.

---

#### 26. Chappanna (Prasna Shastra) — B. Suryanarain Rao (1946)
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/ChappannaOrPrasanaSastraBSuryanarainRao1946Text)

A practical horary astrology text covering the 56 classical rules for Prashna chart reading. Includes the determination of outcome from planetary position at the moment of a query. Suryanarain Rao was the foremost English translator of Jyotish texts in the early 20th century and editor of The Astrological Magazine.

---

### II-F — KP System (Krishnamurti Padhdhati)

---

#### 27. KP Readers I–VI — K.S. Krishnamurti (1900–1972)
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org — full 6-reader series](https://archive.org/details/kp-readers) · [Vol. III](https://archive.org/details/krishnamurti-padhdhati-vol-3-english) · [DLI Vol. I](https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.128044)

The foundational texts of the Krishnamurti Padhdhati system. The KP system uses Placidus houses, a 249-division sub-lord table (sign lord → star lord → sub lord), and a refined significator methodology for predicting the precise timing of events. The six KP Readers cover: (I) casting the horoscope, (II) fundamental principles, (III) predictive stellar astrology, (IV) marriage and children, (V) transits, (VI) horary. All KP features in this application — the sub-lord table, significators, cuspal analysis — derive from these texts.

---

#### 28. Advanced Stellar Astrology — K.S. Krishnamurti
**Tag:** Open · [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.352942)

A supplementary KP text covering advanced applications of the sub-lord system, including medical astrology, finance, and fine-grained event timing. Useful for practitioners who have mastered the six KP Readers.
